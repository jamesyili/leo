## 2026-04-02 — Medvi: AI-Enabled $1.8B Company with 2 Employees

**Link:** https://www.nytimes.com/2026/04/02/technology/ai-billion-dollar-company-medvi.html

**What happened:** Matthew Gallagher built Medvi (telehealth GLP-1 prescriptions) in 2 months with $20k. $401M revenue in 2025, tracking $1.8B in 2026. Two employees (him + brother). Everything operational outsourced — AI used for marketing, code, customer service, analytics.

**Key takeaways for Rekko:**
- AI wasn't the product — it was the execution accelerant. The real advantage was picking a market where demand 100x exceeded supply (GLP-1 weight loss drugs).
- Gallagher spent on distribution (ads, customer acquisition), not product. The product was commodity (online prescription form).
- 16.2% net margin ($65M) vs Hims' 5.5% — operational leverage from outsourcing everything.
- HN criticism: "just an automated prescription mill" — FDA already issued warning letter for false claims and AI-generated before/after photos.
- The lesson for Rekko isn't "use more AI" — it's "find the money pipe." Rekko has sophisticated tech but no feedback loop proving the analysis works and no paying customers.
- Uncomfortable truth: Medvi required willingness to operate in regulatory gray zones. The clean version of this lesson is *speed + market selection + distribution focus* beat *product sophistication*.

## 2026-03-28 — "The New Math of Work" (Ryan Roslansky, LinkedIn CEO)

**Link:** https://www.linkedin.com/pulse/new-math-work-ryan-roslansky-koezc

**Core thesis:** When AI frees up time (1 hour task → 10 minutes), the real question is how workers use the freed time. Two paths: (1) produce more of the same output, or (2) redirect toward contextual judgment and decision-making that machines can't replicate.

**Key ideas:**
- "Judgment and decision-making" = knowledge of specific people, histories, contexts + willingness to challenge assumptions and build new capabilities
- Examples: physicians reading beyond charts, managers diagnosing team dynamics invisible in data, technicians predicting failure through experience
- Democratization: one person over a weekend can now validate ideas that previously required teams and capital
- Old math: expertise deepens through specialization. New math: contextual human judgment is the defensible advantage.
- Critical caveat: "The new math creates real opportunity, but it doesn't create equal opportunity." Access to time, stability, resources remains uneven — requires organizational investment, not just individual initiative.
