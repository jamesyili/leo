# The Conversation You've Been Avoiding

**Source:** https://jeffersonfisher.substack.com/p/the-conversation-youve-been-avoiding
**Ingested:** 2026-04-02
**Tags:** communication, soft-skills

---

Let me ask you something personal.

Is there a conversation you've been putting off? One that lives rent-free in the back of your mind  at dinner, in the shower, right before you fall asleep?

Maybe it's with a parent who doesn't respect your boundaries. A friend who keeps letting you down. A partner you love but can't quite reach lately. A coworker who makes every meeting feel like a battle.

You know the conversation needs to happen. But something keeps stopping you.

I want to talk about that "something" today. Because I think it's more common  and more important  than most people realize.

Why We Avoid Hard Conversations

After years as a trial lawyer and now talking to hundreds of thousands of you every week, I've noticed something: we don't avoid hard conversations because we're weak. We avoid them because we care.

Think about it. You don't lose sleep over conversations with people who don't matter to you. The reason that talk with your sister, or your boss, or your closest friend feels so heavy is because the relationship means something. You're afraid of what might happen if you say the wrong thing. You're afraid of making it worse.

That fear is actually a sign of emotional intelligence. It means you're paying attention.

But here's the problem: avoidance has a cost. Every day you don't have that conversation, the distance grows a little wider. The resentment sits a little deeper. And the version of it you've rehearsed in your head gets a little louder  and usually a lot more dramatic than the real thing would ever be.

What I've Learned About Hard Conversations

I want to share something I don't talk about enough.

I've had my share of conversations I wasn't ready for. Moments where I said the right thing too late, or the wrong thing too soon. Times I stayed quiet when I should have spoken, and spoke when I should have listened.

What I've learned  sometimes the hard way  is that the goal of a hard conversation isn't to win. It's to be understood, and to understand.

When you walk into a difficult conversation trying to be right, you've already lost — not the argument, but the connection. The moment the other person feels attacked, they stop listening. And so do you.

The conversations that actually changed things in my life weren't the ones where I had the perfect words. They were the ones where I was willing to stay in the room and keep going even when it got uncomfortable.

Three Things to Remember Before You Have That Conversation

1. Say what you mean, not what you feel in the moment.

Emotions are real. But leading with them in a heated moment usually makes things worse. Before you speak, ask yourself: What do I actually want from this conversation? Clarity before words.

2. You don't have to have all the answers going in.

One of the biggest myths about difficult conversations is that you have to know exactly what to say. You don't. You just have to be willing to show up honestly and listen as much as you speak.

3. How you say it matters more than what you say.

Your tone, your pace, whether you're leaning in or crossing your arms  all of it communicates something before a single word leaves your mouth. Calm is contagious. So is panic. Choose which one you bring into the room.

A Final Thought

I started sharing communication advice online because I saw how many people were suffering in silence  not because they didn't care, but because they didn't know how to start. How to find the words. How to stay steady when the emotion got high.

If that's you right now, I want you to know something: the fact that you're even thinking about having this conversation already says something good about you.

You don't have to get it perfect. You just have to begin.

That conversation you've been avoiding? It's waiting for you. And it's probably not as impossible as it feels right now.

You've got this.

— Jefferson
