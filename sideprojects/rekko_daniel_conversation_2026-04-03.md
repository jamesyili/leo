# Rekko: Decision Conversation with Daniel

Date: 2026-04-03
Time: 30 minutes
Framework: WRAP (Decisive) — Widen options, Reality-test, Attain distance, Prepare to be wrong

**Opener:** "I want to make sure the massive amount of work we've put into Rekko actually pays off. I've got some structured questions I want us to walk through together — not to be negative, but to make sure we're not blinding ourselves to the best path forward."

---

## Priority 1: What do we actually have? (Moat inventory + reality-test)

Start here. Before deciding what to do next, get honest about what's working and what's genuinely valuable.

### Moat inventory

1. **What have we built that's genuinely hard to replicate?** Not features — capabilities. If we had to start a completely different business tomorrow but could bring three things from Rekko's codebase, what would they be?

2. **Detach the engine from the vertical.** The video pipeline turns structured analysis into published TikTok content. What if prediction markets is just our first *topic*, not our product? What's the highest-engagement topic we could point this engine at tomorrow?

3. **ML portability check.** Our prediction pipeline produces calibrated probability estimates from mixed data. Where else is someone willing to *pay* for calibrated predictions — sports betting, DFS, crypto, enterprise forecasting?

4. **Build vs. bought.** For each of our real moats — could someone replicate this in a weekend with off-the-shelf tools? If yes, it's not a moat. If no, that's where we double down.

### Reality-test

5. **Define the failure signal.** What specific metric or event would have to happen for us to agree that growth hacking the current product isn't working? Let's set that tripwire now, before we're emotionally invested in the next 30 days.

6. **Base-rate check.** If we look at successful prediction market startups, did any of them have this much tech built with zero users? Or did they validate demand early and build after?

7. **Consider the opposite.** Let's assume the tech we've built is entirely the wrong approach for what the market wants. What evidence do we have right now that points to that being true?

---

## Priority 2: Widen options (escape the "which of these three" trap)

Once we know what we actually have, explore where to point it.

8. **Vanishing options test.** If TikTok banned us, APIs didn't work, and shadow trading was illegal — how else could we prove this platform has value?

9. **Opportunity cost.** If we spend the next 30 days growth hacking the current scattered product, what specific learnings are we sacrificing by not running a focused experiment?

10. **Can we multitrack cheaply?** Instead of arguing over which single bet, is there a way to run rapid, low-lift tests on two bets simultaneously this week and let the market decide?

11. **Bigger market test.** The video pipeline is amazing. Can we try pointing it at something more engaging than prediction markets — sports betting picks, stock analysis, crypto narratives, political commentary — and just create AI videos for engagement? That in itself could be a very valuable standalone product.

---

## Priority 3: Attain distance from sunk cost

The hardest part. Daniel is near full-time and has built an enormous amount. The question isn't whether the work was valuable — it's whether it's pointing in the right direction.

12. **The successor question.** If a brand new CEO took over Rekko tomorrow, what would they immediately cut, and which bet would they focus on?

13. **Strategic subtraction.** What feature, codebase, or idea do we need to amputate today so we can actually get one flywheel spinning?

14. **10/10/10.** If we abandon the massive tech pipeline to focus purely on content distribution — how do we feel about that in 10 minutes, 10 months, 10 years?

---

## Priority 4: Prepare to be wrong

If we do pick a bet, build in the safety net.

15. **Pre-mortem.** We go all-in on [chosen bet] for 30 days and it's a complete disaster. What went wrong?

16. **Tripwire.** What's the exact metric — X users, Y trades, Z revenue — that if we don't hit by day 30, we guaranteed stop and pivot?

17. **Market size gut check.** Even if we get traction in prediction markets, is the vertical actually large enough to sustain a business, or is this a niche hobby? Where's the Medvi-equivalent demand signal — demand massively outstripping supply?

---

## For 30 minutes

Focus on Priority 1 (questions 1-7). That's where the real unlock is — getting honest about what's valuable and what's not working. If time allows, move to Priority 2 to explore where to point the moats.

If you run out of time, send Daniel the full list and ask him to sit with it.
